🚫 Important Precautions:

Who Should Avoid Taking This

(Precautions and Contraindications)

1. Pregnant and Breastfeeding Women
  • Some herbs (like cinnamon, dry ginger) may stimulate the uterus during pregnancy.
  • Scientific evidence regarding safety during breastfeeding is limited.
2. Children Under 12 Years of Age
  • Safe dosage for children has not been clearly established.
  • The active compounds may have a stronger effect on their bodies.
3. Patients with Autoimmune Diseases

(Examples: Rheumatoid arthritis, Lupus, Multiple sclerosis)

  • Guduchi and Tulsi may over-activate the immune system, potentially worsening autoimmune symptoms.
4. People on Blood-Thinning Medication

(Examples: Warfarin, Aspirin)

  • Amla, Cinnamon, and Black Pepper may enhance blood-thinning effects, increasing bleeding risk.
5. Low Blood Sugar (Hypoglycemia) or Diabetes Patients
  • Cinnamon and Guduchi may lower blood sugar levels.
  • If you are already on diabetes medication, your sugar level could drop too low.
6. People with Bile Disorders or Heartburn
  • Dry Ginger (Sunthi) and Black Pepper may increase bile, potentially worsening heartburn or acidity.
7. Patients with Severe Liver or Kidney Disease
  • Herbal extracts are metabolized in the liver and kidneys, which may put extra load on these organs.
8. People with Allergies
  • If you are allergic to any of these herbs (Tulsi, Cinnamon, Ginger, etc.), you may experience rashes, itching, or difficulty breathing.

If you have any chronic illness, are on medication, or are pregnant, always consult a doctor or Ayurvedic practitioner before use.
