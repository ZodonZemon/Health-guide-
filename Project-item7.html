üö´ Important Precautions:

1. Patients with Autoimmune Diseases

    Examples: Rheumatoid arthritis, Lupus (SLE), Multiple sclerosis

    Reason: Giloy can over-activate the immune system, which may worsen the symptoms of such diseases.

2. People Taking Autoimmune Medications

    In people taking immunosuppressive drugs (e.g., after organ transplant), Giloy may reduce the effectiveness of the medicine.

3. Pregnant Women

    It may affect the baby in the womb and cause hormonal changes, so it should be avoided during pregnancy.

4. Breastfeeding Women

    There is not enough research about its effects on infants through breast milk.

5. People with Low Blood Sugar (Hypoglycemia)

    Giloy may further lower blood sugar levels.

    Diabetes patients already on medication should take it with caution.

6. People with Severe Liver Disease

    Some reports suggest that excessive consumption of Giloy may increase liver enzyme levels.

7. Patients with Severe Kidney Conditions

    Taking large amounts may put extra strain on the kidneys.

8. People with Drug Allergies

    If you have had allergies to herbal or Ayurvedic medicines in the past, avoid taking Giloy without a test dose.

üìå Note:

Always consult an Ayurvedic practitioner or doctor before taking any herbal medicine for a long duration.

The effect of Giloy may vary from person to person, so it is important to follow the correct dosage and duration.
